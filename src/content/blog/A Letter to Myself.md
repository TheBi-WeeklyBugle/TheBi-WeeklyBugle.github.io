I’m about to graduate high school; I’ve already turned 18, so this feels as good of a time as any to write this. Today wasn’t your first time falling short, and it definitely isn’t going to be the last, but take that anger and burn it; use it as fuel and fly higher. 10 years from now, I’m expecting great things. 

If the first 18 years of life are but for Harvard, holy shit, you’ve failed. The culmination of your whole life, nearly two decades, was blown through in 5 minutes; Shakespeare couldn’t have written a greater tragedy. You’ve truly done it all; you can do it all, but what’s there to show? This last year has certainly been frustrating, but that’s the way it goes, right? Nothing easy is ever fun; what the hell’s the point, then? Are there regrets? Sure, but why cry over spilt milk? 

You’re not the same person you were a year ago—hell, you’re not the same person you were yesterday—but promise me one thing: never lose that drive. 10 years is always when you say you’ll be someone that’s something—prove it. Never settle, never quit, and never stay down; go over, around, or through.
